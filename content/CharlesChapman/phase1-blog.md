# Leuven, Belgium

In my first week here in Leuven, what stands out as being the most fascinating thing to me is the history. Growing up in the US, our history is not nearly as long or well-documented as European history. Knowing that the cobblestones underneath my feet are hundreds of years old, or seeing a building that was bombed in WW2, or visiting the Béguinage that housed Beguines for hundreds of years, I feel the content that I learned in high school history class is coming alive around me.

# Phase 1 Contributions

Within this process, I have brainstormed topic ideas, explored datasets from Eurostat and other sources, and completed a user persona to help with our planning and design process. # Course Content

I have found the course content of CS3200 really interesting so far. I have worked with SQL very limitedly in the past, primarily for performing SQL injection attacks in CTFs. I have found it very helpful to gain a deeper understanding of what those SQL injection payloads do by learning how to use SQL to interact with a database as a whole. Furthermore, I have really enjoyed the SELECT puzzles we have been completing in class as I feel it forces to me problem-solve in a new way.

# Trips and Guest Speakers

I have found the trips and guest speakers, specifically those discussing the European Union, to be very helpful in understanding how the EU functions and the role it plays in European governance. I have always been somewhat confused about what the EU was and its authority over the European nations. This past semester I took a class on International Relations in which we discussed the IR framework of Realism. Realism states that international institutions are essentially pointless as they have no real authority over the individual nations. However, these guest speakers addressed this point to explain how the EU is able to maintain more authority over its member nations than any other international institution such as the United Nations.
